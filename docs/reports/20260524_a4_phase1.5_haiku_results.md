## TL;DR

- **The Phase 1 A4 signal (+0.27 tier) did not replicate at higher N.** Phase 1.5 finds A4 Δ = **−0.12** tier vs C0, 95% CI [−0.45, +0.18]. CI is tight enough (±0.3 vs Phase 1's ±1.0) to say: A4 has essentially no positive effect on Haiku. The G1 gate fails.
- **A4 does NOT actively regress overall.** The safety bound holds (H2 Δ = −0.24 on oscillation, threshold −0.3).
- **A4 reproduces the Phase 1 "foundation-miss" pattern on this bench too.** On `oscillation-bfb84ae55c3f` and `early-e6291973dd07`, A4 picked a non-foundation move on a turn where C0 took the foundation 3/3 times. Restricted to the 4 oscillation-with-foundation states, A4 Δ = **−0.42** [−1.33, +0.17]. Same failure mode as A2 in Phase 1.
- **Recommendation per pre-registered decision rule**: **DEFER A4.** H1 failed but H2 holds, so the rule says "wait for `de7dc06` production data to accumulate, then re-evaluate." Do not graduate A4 to Phase 2 Gemma on this evidence.

## What was actually run

- **120 calls**: 20 states × 2 arms × 3 runs each, all via Claude Haiku 4.5 subagent simulation
- **Bench**: 20 post-cutover states (template hash `0462323c…`) — 5 early / 8 midgame / 0 endgame / 7 oscillation. **Endgame was unavailable** because both post-cutover sessions stalled at ≤21% progress.
- **Oscillation-with-foundation**: 4 of 7 oscillation states had a `tableau_to_foundation` move available — the H2 test ground.
- **Pairing**: every (state, run) has a matched C0/A4 pair. Paired bootstrap with 5000 resamples for CI.

### The foundation-miss pattern (smoking gun, replicated)

On the 4 oscillation states with `tableau_to_foundation` available — the same pattern that bit A2 in Phase 1 (`oscillation-bffb5d9cc178`) — A4 underperforms C0 by **−0.42 tier** [95% CI −1.33, +0.17]. The largest single-state loss:

| state | category | C0 picks | C0 tiers | A4 picks | A4 tiers |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `oscillation-bfb84ae55c3f` | osc/found | [1, 1, 1] | foundation × 3 (**6.00**) | [1, 1, 2] | foundation × 2, shuffle × 1 (**4.33**) |

On both states, the prompt offered a `tableau_to_foundation` move that C0 picked unanimously. A4 missed it on 1 of 3 runs each time. n is tiny (2 states), but it's the *same* failure pattern as A2 in Phase 1, on a *different* bench, in a *different* arm. That makes it worth flagging as a real risk, not a fluke.

## Interpretation: Phase 1 vs Phase 1.5

| Metric | Phase 1 (N=15) | Phase 1.5 (N=60) | Change |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| A4 Δ vs C0 | +0.27 | −0.12 | Sign flip |
| 95% CI width | ±0.83 | ±0.31 | ~3× tighter |
| A4 wins on oscillation? | 3.33 vs C0 3.83 (lost) | 3.00 vs C0 3.24 (lost) | Same direction |
| A4 best mean tier? | Yes (3.93) | No (C0 wins 2.52 > A4 2.40) | Reversed |

**The Phase 1 result was inside the Phase 1.5 CI.** That's the cleanest possible interpretation: Phase 1 estimated a wide-CI positive effect; Phase 1.5 tightened the CI and found the true value was near zero, likely mildly negative. This is the textbook outcome when a low-N pilot suggests a small effect — most of the time the effect was noise.

**The Phase 1 audit prediction (+0.0 to +0.2)** is also within the Phase 1.5 CI [−0.45, +0.18]. So the audit was directionally right that A4 wouldn't be *strongly* negative, but the small positive it predicted didn't materialize on Haiku.

## Pre-registered decision: DEFER A4

Per the test plan's decision rules:

> **If H1 fails but H2 holds (no oscillation regression)**: Defer A4. The Haiku data is consistent with A4 being mildly positive but not strongly so. Wait for `de7dc06` production data to accumulate (target: ≥10 post-cutover sessions, so we have an apples-to-apples comparison with pre-cutover gameplay). Re-evaluate then.

H1 failed (Δ = −0.12, CI [−0.45, +0.18]; the data is mildly *against* A4, not just neutral). H2 passed (Δ = −0.24 ≥ −0.3 safety threshold). So the rule says: **defer**. Do not burn Gemma quota on A4 yet.

We have 2 post-cutover sessions in `data/raw/` right now. Need ~8 more before the observational analysis becomes useful.

## Caveats

- **Haiku is not Gemma.** A4 could still help on Gemma — Haiku may already be using internal reasoning to compensate for notation placement, while Gemma may benefit from the cleaner preamble. But: that's now a *speculative* upside, not one we have evidence for.
- **n = 7 oscillation states is still small.** The oscillation-with-foundation CI [−1.33, +0.17] crosses 0; we can't statistically distinguish "A4 mildly worse on this slice" from "A4 substantially worse." Either is possible.
- **No endgame states.** H3 was untestable because both post-cutover sessions stalled before endgame. The Phase 1 endgame finding (A4 hits 6.00) remains unreplicated.
- **Subagent simulation overhead** is the same as Phase 1; same caveats about identity bleed.
- **Output-size +6.6% is suspicious but not actionable.** Most likely an artifact of Haiku's slightly different prose generation when notation is in the preamble vs inline. Would not replicate on Gemma necessarily.

## What this means for the harvester team

Three observations worth surfacing:

1. **The A1 change they shipped (`reasoningTrail` removal) has parallel evidence problems.** Phase 1 said A1 was directionally negative on Haiku (Δ = −0.53). They shipped it anyway. We now have 2 post-cutover sessions — once we have ~10 we can directly compare pre/post-cutover game quality. If A1 actually hurt production play, that's the most actionable finding.

2. **A4 (notation hoist) does not look worth shipping on this evidence.** Originally I framed A4 as the strongest tier-score candidate. Phase 1.5 says that was overcalled at N=15. They should not adopt A4 based on the audit alone.

3. **The discovered `PRIOR REASONING` section is essentially the renamed `reasoningTrail` anti-pattern.** During bench-building I noticed the new `0462323c…` template still ships the model's prior step-by-step reasonings at the END of every prompt — just renamed and reformatted. This is the same anchoring failure pattern the audit flagged. The A1 fix removed the JSON key but did not remove the underlying behavior. Worth raising with the harvester team — there may be more reduction available here than from any A2/A4 layout change.

## Approach notes (Phase 1.5 lessons)

- **N=60 (20×3) is a much better signal than N=15 (5×3) for this kind of test.** CI shrank from ±0.83 to ±0.31. Phase 2 designs should default to ≥20 states.
- **The bench has to come from the same template as production**, or you're measuring template-shift confounds. We had to drop the original 4 endgame quota because post-cutover sessions don't reach endgame — accept the smaller bench rather than mix templates.
- **Two-arm beats multi-arm when you have a focused question.** All 120 of our calls went into the A4-vs-C0 contrast. Spreading across 5 arms (Phase 1) wasted power on questions we already had data for.
- **Pre-registered decision rules save a lot of rationalization.** "Defer" is the unambiguous output of H1-fail + H2-pass. Without it I'd be tempted to spin the +0.18 upper CI bound into a Phase 2 graduation. Don't.
- **Surface the smoking gun even when it's marginal.** The oscillation-bfb84a foundation miss is n=1 on a 60-call experiment. But it replicates the *same anti-pattern* as A2's `oscillation-bffb5d9cc178` from Phase 1 — different state, different arm, same shape. That cross-arm cross-state replication is meaningful even if neither single observation is significant.
